I am an Associate Professor of Economics and Co-Chair of Finance at Gettysburg College. I am an applied microeconomist and policy analyst with research interests in Public Economics, Labor Economics, and Experimental Economics.

My work focuses on social and economic policy issues, including retirement security, tax policy, welfare programs, and labor market outcomes. I have conducted research on topics ranging from discrimination in labor markets to environmental economics, with particular expertise in the intersection of public policy and economic mobility.

I received my Ph.D. in Economics from the University of California, Berkeley in 2001, and hold an M.P.I.A. in Economic and Social Development from the University of Pittsburgh. Prior to my academic career, I gained valuable policy experience working as an Economist at the U.S. General Accounting Office and The Urban Institute.

Research Interests

Public Finance

Tax policy, government spending, and fiscal policy analysis with focus on distributional effects and economic mobility.

Development Economics

Economic development policies, poverty reduction strategies, and international development programs.

Labor Economics

Wage discrimination, sexual orientation and labor outcomes, and employment effects of social policies.

Social Security & Retirement

Retirement income adequacy, Social Security policy, and intergenerational wealth transfers.

Environmental Economics

Environmental Kuznets curves, environmental policy, and sustainable development.

Behavioral Economics

Experimental economics, decision-making under uncertainty, and overconfidence in economic choices.
